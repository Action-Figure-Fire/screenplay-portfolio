Synopsis

DOUGHNUT SUNDAY is a multi-layered dramedy set in a "still in the '50s" steel town where a Catholic parish decides to take the law into their own hands — strictly within biblical guidelines, of course.

For Holden and Mary Catherine, it's a coming-of-age story. Their backgrounds couldn't be more different, yet they ask the same question: "What's next?" For Mrs. Opal, it's survival — her husband's at war, her daughter's growing up, and her neighborhood's falling apart. If she doesn't hold it together, her husband won't have anything to come back to.

The pilot plays heavily on the church's tropes, witnessed through Holden's eyes. He acts as the lens of society, picking the low-hanging fruit without considering the roots that made it possible — slowly morphing to see the beauty while still laughing at the absurdity.

And for everyone else? It's pure comedy. Sit back and watch the dumpster fire — full of all religions, cultures, and societies — burn gloriously together.
