QUIETUS

When U.S. domination lies in the unquestioned extermination of Americans sixty and older, one CEO discovers his company built the machine — and must decide whether to run or burn it all down.

During a devastating depression, America enacts the Quietus — mandatory death at 60 — transforming the nation into a youthful superpower while the world rots. Benjamin Braverly, the new CEO of Winchester Finance, believes the Resistance killed his family. He's wrong about everything. When Winchester puts a hit on their own CEO, Benjamin flees into the extermination facilities he helped run — and emerges as the rebellion's most dangerous weapon.

DOUGHNUT SUNDAY

When church parishioners get fed up with the crime in their small town, they form a vigilante group — inflicting justice according to what the Bible allows them. What could possibly go wrong?

Set in a steel town frozen in the 1950s, a Catholic parish decides to take the law into their own hands — within biblical guidelines, of course. It's a coming-of-age story for Holden and Mary Catherine, a survival story for Mrs. Opal (whose husband's at war), and pure comedic chaos for everyone else. Think nativity-costume disguises, "holy water" that's actually gasoline, and a second collection plate funding covert operations.

STUCK

Stop the divorce — they've run out of money!

Tyler and Vivi are ten years married when everything implodes. A wham-bam divorce filing gets frozen by a job crisis and $20K missing from their account, forcing them to cohabitate through the wreckage. Tyler stumbles between saving his marriage and saving his career. Vivi discovers the culinary world — and a version of herself her marriage never allowed. With the help of their deeply unhelpful best friends, both learn that love isn't about holding on. It's about knowing when to let go.

SCAVENGERS

Becoming an adult at 18 isn't easy — especially when it involves police chases, sociopathic girlfriends, stabbings (not a 911 kinda stabbing, more of a "quiet Sunday night" kinda stabbing), abandoned houses, and a city-wide scavenger hunt.

On the last night of his house arrest, 18-year-old Benji watches his friends leave for college while he can't even get accepted. After catching his psychotic girlfriend cheating, stabbing his mom's boyfriend, and fleeing to an abandoned house in the woods, he somehow finds a path forward — only to have it demolished by his nemesis Troy. The deal: win a city-wide scavenger hunt or lose everything. The cost: jumping off a hundred-foot bridge from a moving train. The ending: a burned-down house and a Marine Corps pamphlet.
