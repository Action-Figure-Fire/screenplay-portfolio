# PART 2: WHERE TO SEND YOUR MATERIAL

## A. THE BLACK LIST (blcklst.com) — START HERE

**Why it's #1:** Industry professionals actively browse it. An 8/10 score gets you free hosting and real attention from agents/managers.

**How it works:**
- Upload script → $30/month hosting
- Pay $100 per professional evaluation (scored 1-10 with written feedback)
- Scripts scoring 8+ get: free hosting, free additional evaluations, featured placement
- Hundreds of industry pros (agents, managers, producers) browse weekly
- Real success: Writer scored 8/10, cold-emailed managers referencing the score, signed with Empirical Evidence management firm (2024)

**Your strategy:**
1. Host **Quietus** first (highest concept, most commercial)
2. Get 2-3 evaluations — if 7+, keep it up and start querying with the score
3. If 8+, you're in the fast lane — managers will find YOU
4. Budget: ~$230-$330 to start (1 month hosting + 2-3 evals)

## B. STAGE 32 — PITCH DIRECTLY TO EXECS

**What it is:** Online platform where you pitch LIVE to real producers, development execs, and managers via Zoom/phone.

**Real success stories:**
- Writer optioned "MERRILY ALL THE WAY" (Holiday Rom-Com) through a Stage 32 pitch to Collab Content Entertainment — now in development
- Writer Aaron Benjamin signed with a manager directly from a pitch session
- Writer from New Zealand got script into development with Night and Day Pictures via Stage 32 pitchfest

**How to use it:**
- Browse pitch sessions by genre ($35-75 per pitch)
- Pick execs who match your genre
- You get 5-10 minutes to pitch + feedback
- If they like it, they request the script

**Your play:**
- Pitch **Doughnut Sunday** to TV execs (pilot-ready, great hook)
- Pitch **Stuck** to rom-com producers (the logline alone will get a laugh)

## C. INKTIP — PRODUCERS COME TO YOU

**What it is:** Database where producers search for scripts by genre/logline. 400+ movies made from InkTip scripts. 3,000+ options.

**Cost:** $65/4 months for Pro membership (script listing + weekly pitch requests)

**Best for:** Lower-budget indie projects → **Scavengers** and **Stuck** are perfect fits

**How to maximize:**
- Respond to their weekly "Preferred Newsletter" pitch requests (producers post what they're looking for)
- Keep your loglines tight and genre-specific
- Update your listing every few months with fresh loglines

## D. COVERFLY — YOUR TRACKING BOARD

**What it is:** Free platform that tracks your contest placements and creates a "writer score" visible to industry.

**Why it matters:** Agents and managers browse Coverfly's Red List (top-ranked writers from competitions)

**Action:** Create a profile, link all contest entries, build your score

# PART 3: CONTESTS THAT ACTUALLY MATTER

## Tier 1 — Career Launchers (Submit to ALL of these)

### 1. NICHOLL FELLOWSHIP (Academy of Motion Picture Arts & Sciences)
- **Prize:** Up to 5 fellowships × $35,000 each
- **Window:** Usually April-May annually
- **Why:** THE most prestigious screenwriting award. Semifinalists get agent attention. Winners get meetings at every studio.
- **Submit:** Quietus, Scavengers, Stuck (features only)

### 2. PAGE INTERNATIONAL SCREENWRITING AWARDS
- **Prize:** $25,000 Grand Prize + prizes in 10 genre categories
- **Why:** Genre-specific judging means your thriller competes against thrillers, not rom-coms
- **Categories for you:** Thriller (Quietus), Comedy (Stuck), Drama (Scavengers), TV Pilot (Doughnut Sunday)
- **Website:** pageawards.com

### 3. AUSTIN FILM FESTIVAL (AFF)
- **Conference:** October 29 - November 5, 2026
- **Why:** The conference is where the real action is — agents, managers, producers all attend. Winners and finalists get meetings.
- **Bonus:** Enderby Entertainment offers meetings to select winners
- **Submit:** All four scripts (they have feature + TV categories)

### 4. FINAL DRAFT BIG BREAK
- **Why:** Backed by Final Draft (industry standard software) — winners get serious visibility
- **Submit:** All scripts

## Tier 2 — Worth Entering

### 5. ATLANTA FILM FESTIVAL SCREENPLAY COMPETITION
- **Deadline:** November 2025 cycle (check for 2026)
- **Via Coverfly**

### 6. SCREENCRAFT (Multiple genre-specific contests)
- Various deadlines throughout the year
- Genre-specific = better odds

### 7. SCRIPT SUMMIT
- Includes InkTip Pro membership as a prize

# PART 7: OUTREACH STRATEGY & CADENCE

## The 90-Day Campaign

### Week 1-2: Preparation
- [ ] Upload Quietus to The Black List ($30 + $100 for first eval)
- [ ] Create Coverfly profile, link all scripts
- [ ] Sign up for InkTip Pro ($65/4 months)
- [ ] Get IMDbPro ($20/month) for research
- [ ] Build target list of 30 agents/managers (genre-matched)

### Week 3-4: Contest Submissions
- [ ] Submit to Nicholl (if in window), PAGE, Austin, Big Break
- [ ] Enter Doughnut Sunday in TV-specific contests
- [ ] Apply to any open TV fellowships

### Week 5-8: Query Campaign
- [ ] Send 10-15 personalized queries per week (not mass blasts)
- [ ] Lead with the script that matches each contact's taste
- [ ] Track all responses in a spreadsheet
- [ ] Follow up on non-responses after 2-3 weeks

### Week 9-12: Stage 32 + Expand
- [ ] Book 3-5 Stage 32 pitch sessions ($35-75 each)
- [ ] Pitch Doughnut Sunday to TV execs
- [ ] Pitch Stuck to rom-com producers
- [ ] Evaluate Black List scores — if 7+, reference in all queries

### Ongoing
- [ ] Respond to InkTip weekly pitch requests
- [ ] Engage on X/Twitter #screenwriting community
- [ ] Monitor Coverfly score
- [ ] Enter new contests as they open

## Rules of Engagement
1. **ONE script per contact** — mention you have others if asked
2. **Personalize every query** — reference their clients or recent deals
3. **Never send the script unsolicited** — always ask permission first
4. **Follow up once** after 2-3 weeks, then move on
5. **Track everything** — who, when, which script, response
6. **The "almost sold" line is GOLD** — use it in every query
