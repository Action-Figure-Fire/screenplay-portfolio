Synopsis

Everyone remembers that kid from high school — the one on a permanent crash course with no helmet, no brakes, and probably full of uppers after taking downers. SCAVENGERS is that kid. Except this time, that kid painfully learns to break the mold.

The story begins on the last night of Benji's house arrest. He's had his share of run-ins with the local PD, but now he's eighteen with a clean slate. Instead of playing it safe, he gets hammered and goes off-roading while his friends ditch his party for college freshman orientation.

His friends stage an intervention — urging him to prep for college, stop the self-destruction, and above all, dump Tara: that girl every teenage boy needs to avoid but can't. They think it's love but it's really Stockholm syndrome. What they don't know: he still hasn't gotten into college, and Tara has a year of high school left.

When Benji catches Tara cheating with Troy, the spiral accelerates — a confrontation, cops, a drunken fight at home with his mother's boyfriend that ends with a shard of glass and a panicked run into the woods. He holes up in an abandoned house to figure out his life.

And somehow, in those woods, he does. With his old football coach's help, he finds a path: prep school, football, a second chance. But on the drive back, Troy finds him. Another fight. Cops. Benji punches an officer and flees back to his hideout.

Troy shows up with a deal: he has video proving Benji was jumped — but he'll only hand it over if Benji enters a city-wide scavenger hunt. Knowing Troy expects him to get arrested mid-game, Benji agrees anyway. One last night with his friends before prison.

The hunt escalates until every cop in town is chasing them. With the score tied, Benji does the one thing nobody will: he hops a moving train and jumps off a hundred-foot bridge. He wins. He's arrested. And the next day, he's free — having cut a deal with the police chief. His friends meet him at the house in the woods. He burns it down. Then he throws a pamphlet on the table: U.S. MARINES.

"What do you do when you can't get into college, your friends are leaving, and your mom kicks you out? You move into an abandoned house in the woods and pray your life magically comes together."
